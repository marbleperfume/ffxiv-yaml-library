## 🩹 HEALING & RECOVERY

> **Healing System Decisions (from game reference analysis):**
> - Estus/Crimson Tear model = fixed "healing budget" per attempt rather than infinite sustain
> - Goodberry model = granular out-of-combat healing that's action-efficient but slot-inefficient
> - The strategic function "Burst Healing" is what Healer classes replace as premium alternatives

### Game Reference Items

## 🧪 CC/DEBUFF — Status Effects, Crowd Control & Enemy Manipulation

> **CC/Debuff Design Context (from debuff expansion):**
> - Strategy (items/consumables) comes BEFORE class design. Classes are "premium replacements."
> - The Shaman class replaces debuff/CC items as its strategic role.
> - Three strategic axes: Map Position (x,y,z), Action Availability (resource gates, cooldowns), Time to Achieve Action (cast commitment, opportunity cost).
> - Enemy AI uses Strategy Rank 1–4. Items matter most at SR2–3.
> - All items are target agnostic — no "humanoid only" restrictions.
> - Paralysis model: speed reduction + % chance to skip action.

## DESIGN PRINCIPLES (Preserved from Source 1)

1. **Carry limits create decisions** — Monster Hunter's limited pouch means you CAN'T bring everything. Force choices pre-mission.
2. **Combo items reward knowledge** — Tranq Bomb is worthless without a Trap. Dragon's Dream needs a fire source. Let items synergize.
3. **Shared AoE buffs reward proximity** — Demon Powder only helps nearby allies. Spatial play emerges from item design.
4. **Per-encounter use caps** — Flash Pod's 3-use limit means timing matters more than ownership.
5. **Dual-edge items create tension** — Ember/Dried Finger give power AND exposure. Dung Pie poisons you too.
6. **Environment-reactive items** — Fire Bombs igniting oil puddles. Let items interact with the world state.
7. **Items that change fight STRUCTURE** — Screamer Pod forces a flying monster to land. Don't just buff numbers; change geometry.
8. **Team resource tension** — DRG's resupply is shared among 4 players. Force communication about allocation.
9. **Knowledge-gated value** — Boreal Coat, Nullberry, specific Trap types are worthless unless you know the encounter. Reward preparation.
10. **Investment vs. immediate use** — White Gull, Rare Candy, Seed of Life force "use now or save" decisions that never fully resolve.

## INSTALLATION LIFECYCLE RULES (Preserved from Source 2)

All installations (traps, totems, lanterns, devices) share these rules:
- Placed with a **1–2s deployment action** (player is committed during placement)
- Persist for stated duration OR until destroyed
- SR1–2 enemies **ignore** installations (don't target them)
- SR3 enemies **contest** installations (attack them when nearby, may path to destroy)
- SR4 enemies **prioritize** installations (destroy them immediately, may bypass other targets)
- Destroyed installations are CONSUMED (not recoverable)
