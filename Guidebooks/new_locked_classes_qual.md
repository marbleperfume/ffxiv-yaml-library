# New Racially Locked Classes — Qualitative Context Document

## Lock Type Definitions

### Biological Lock
The race's body **IS** the class mechanic. The class cannot be played without that specific physiology — the abilities emerge from what the body physically does, not from learned technique. Remove the body, remove the class entirely.

### Cultural Lock
The race's tradition **IS** the class knowledge. The knowledge is socially exclusive — outsiders aren't taught, don't have context, or lack the cultural framework to execute. The body isn't strictly required; the gatekeeping is social/traditional rather than physiological.

---

## CC Stage System

### Stage 1 — Slows, Roots, Vision Reduction
- Don't stop attacks in range
- Favors ranged attackers (target can't close distance but can still fight back)
- Lowest commitment to apply

### Stage 2 — Silences, Interrupts, Confusion
- Stop or prevent specific actions
- Favors everyone at specific tactical moments (shutting down a cast, preventing a heal)
- Medium commitment to apply

### Stage 3 — Stuns, Blinds, Paralysis, Sleep
- Complete or significant disable
- **ALWAYS** good — force multiplier with teammates
- Games add resistances to prevent permanent lockdown — we will too
- Highest commitment to apply

### Design Principles
- Higher stage = more DPS lost to apply OR longer cooldown
- CC is amplified by teammates/summons (a stun is worth more when allies capitalize)
- Stage determines the class's control budget — Grappler melee stance is Stage 3, which is WHY it's a frontline commitment class

## 1. Siren / Cantor

**Race:** Merryin  
**Tier:** Experienced  
**Lock Type:** Biological (vocal apparatus produces frequencies other races physically cannot)  
**Role:** Healer / CC  

### Strategic Identity
The "I force you to do X" class. Attention override — the Siren's voice doesn't suppress, degrade, or debuff. It **commands**. Enemies are forced into specific actions against their will. This is not Unfocus (which suppresses targeting priority), not Shaman (which degrades stats passively), and not Elementalist (which reactively triages). The Siren dictates enemy behavior directly.

### What It Replaces (Consumable Baseline)
Consumables that redirect aggro, taunt items, confusion bombs. The Siren does this as a persistent, scalable class identity rather than a one-off item effect.

### Input Modality / Fantasy
Singing as spellcasting. The player is a conductor of the battlefield — choosing which enemies do what, when. The fantasy is the mythological siren: irresistible, compelling, impossible to ignore once heard.

### Key Mechanics (Conceptual)
- Voice-based abilities that override enemy action queues
- Commands force movement, force attacks on specific targets, force ability usage at bad times
- Lightning/water racial elements woven into vocalization effects
- Healing delivered through harmonic resonance (allied frequency vs. enemy override frequency)
- Distinct from traditional CC: not "you can't act" but "you MUST act how I say"

### How the Race's Body IS the Class
Merryin vocal cords produce frequencies that interface with neurological attention systems. Other races can sing — Merryin singing **overwrites cognition**. The biology isn't "good singing voice," it's "vocal apparatus that produces compulsion-frequency sound waves." No other throat can do this.

### Open Questions
- How does command interact with boss immunity? Partial override? Reduced duration? Forced sub-optimal action rather than full puppet?
- Healing modality — is it the same voice simultaneously healing allies and commanding enemies, or does the Siren switch between modes?
- How distinct does "command enemy to attack their ally" feel from a standard confusion debuff in practice?
- Interaction with Siren's own lightning/water elements — do commands carry elemental payload?

## 2. Siegebreaker

**Race:** Wyrpincers  
**Tier:** Base  
**Lock Type:** Biological (exoskeleton + mass = the armor and the weapon)  
**Role:** Frontline  

### Strategic Identity
Pure physical, commitment-based. The "lazy race that rewards simple play" identity — high floor, low ceiling. Once committed to a direction, the Siegebreaker is unstoppable but cannot redirect. Simple gameplay by design: STR 18 provides raw force, exoskeleton provides armor through attacks (not dodging, not blocking — just tanking), SPD 5 means slow but inevitable. NTU 3 means this is NOT a reaction-based class. MAGICCAP 6 means banned from all casting — this is muscle and chitin, nothing else.

### What It Replaces (Consumable Baseline)
Heavy armor consumables, fortification potions, damage reduction items. The Siegebreaker's body IS the consumable — it doesn't need external protection because it IS external protection.

### Input Modality / Fantasy
Minimal input complexity. The fantasy is a battering ram — you point it at a wall and it goes through. Player skill is in commitment decisions (when to go, where to go), not mechanical execution. The reward loop is "I made one good decision and it paid off enormously" rather than "I executed a 12-step combo perfectly."

### Key Mechanics (Conceptual)
- Super armor through attacks (doesn't flinch, doesn't stop, absorbs while advancing)
- Commitment-based movement — once engaged, direction is locked or near-locked
- Exoskeleton provides passive damage reduction that scales with forward momentum
- No casting, no reactions, no complex rotations
- Punishes hesitation in others — if you don't move, Siegebreaker reaches you

### How the Race's Body IS the Class
Wyrpincer exoskeleton isn't armor — it's their skin. Their mass isn't gear — it's their body. STR 18 isn't trained — it's baseline physiology. You cannot make a non-Wyrpincer into a Siegebreaker because the class IS "be a Wyrpincer and walk forward." The exoskeleton absorbs, the mass creates momentum, the pincers break what's in front. There's no technique to teach — it's what the body does when it moves with intent.

### Open Questions
- How does "commitment" work mechanically? Charge-and-lock? Directional momentum that penalizes turning?
- What keeps Siegebreaker relevant when enemies simply avoid them? Does the threat of commitment force positioning from opponents?
- At Base tier, how does low ceiling manifest? Do they simply not scale as hard as Experienced classes?
- How does SPD 5 interact with initiative/turn order? Always last but always devastating when they arrive?

## 3. Grappler

**Race:** Iblis Grabbers  
**Tier:** Special  
**Lock Type:** Biological (tentacle offhand IS the class — no tentacle, no grapple system)  
**Role:** Frontline  

### Strategic Identity
The predator who PREPARES their hunt type before entering. Stance-locked tentacle offhand with 4 stances that determine gameplay expression entirely. Stances are chosen pre-instance and cannot be swapped mid-run — the Grappler decides what kind of predator they are TODAY, then commits fully. Main hand carries conventional weaponry (flail, dual kama, sickle+chain); the offhand slot IS the tentacle (equippable augment that modifies stance behavior).

### What It Replaces (Consumable Baseline)
Grappling hooks, nets, restraint items, smoke bombs (ink stance). The tentacle replaces an entire category of utility consumables by being a persistent, stance-modified appendage.

### Input Modality / Fantasy
Predator preparation. The fantasy is the ambush predator who decides their hunting style before the hunt — not adaptive in the moment, but devastatingly effective when the preparation matches the situation. The player's skill is in READING the upcoming content and choosing the right stance.

### Key Mechanics (Conceptual)
- **Stealth Stance:** Ambush opener — tentacle concealed until first strike, massive initiation advantage
- **Long-range Stance:** Extend/throw tentacle to initiate from distance — gap close, displacement, pull-in
- **Ink Stance:** Area denial, vision reduction — tentacle sprays ink for zone control and CC (Stage 1 vision reduction)
- **Melee Grapple Stance:** Stage 3 CC — full pin, non-negotiable disable. This is WHY it's frontline (Stage 3 justifies the commitment)
- Stances locked pre-instance — preparation IS the skill expression
- Main hand + tentacle offhand = dual-system combat (conventional weapon + biological tool)

### How the Race's Body IS the Class
Iblis Grabbers have tentacles. The tentacle IS the offhand slot — not a weapon equipped in the offhand, but the offhand itself being a biological appendage. The stances represent different ways the tentacle can be configured/prepared (coiled for ambush, extended for reach, engorged for ink, tensed for grip). No other race has a functional tentacle offhand. The class is literally "fight with your extra limb."

### Open Questions
- How does stance-lock interact with party composition? Is the Grappler expected to "call" their stance based on party needs?
- Tentacle as equippable augment — does this mean tentacle upgrades/modifications exist as gear? Tentacle enchantments?
- If stance is locked pre-instance, what happens in open-world content? Stance locked per session? Per combat?
- How does Special tier manifest? Higher skill ceiling than Base but different access requirements?
- Melee grapple Stage 3 — does full pin immobilize the Grappler too, or only the target?

## 4. Spiritcaller / Tidebinder

**Race:** Lower Drakols  
**Tier:** Experienced  
**Lock Type:** Biological (Water Slime is innate racial companion — not summoned, BORN WITH)  
**Role:** Healer  

### Strategic Identity
The "healer that isn't a caster." The Water Slime is an INNATE racial companion — not summoned through magic, not conjured, not temporary. It exists because Lower Drakols generate water (BonusSkill: GenerateWater) and the slime is a symbiotic organism that has always been there. The class builds on directing the slime for scouting, healing, or damage while the Drakol fights conventionally. Distinct input modality: directing a persistent companion rather than casting heal spells.

### What It Replaces (Consumable Baseline)
Healing potions, scouting familiars, water-breathing items. The slime IS the healing delivery system, the scout, and the water generator — all consumable categories replaced by a permanent biological companion.

### Input Modality / Fantasy
Companion direction. The player fights as the Drakol (melee/physical) while issuing directives to the slime (heal target, scout area, absorb damage, attack). The fantasy is the warrior with a symbiotic partner — not a summoner commanding minions, but a duo where both halves are always present. The skill expression is in multitasking: fight AND direct simultaneously.

### Key Mechanics (Conceptual)
- Water Slime is always present — not summoned, not on cooldown, not dismissable
- Slime receives directives: heal (attach to ally, transfer water/life), scout (extend through spaces Drakol can't reach), damage (engulf, dissolve, drown)
- Drakol fights conventionally while slime operates semi-independently
- GenerateWater BonusSkill feeds the slime (resource generation for companion)
- Healing delivered through slime contact — physical healing, not magical casting
- Only race with an innate companion that forms the basis of a class

### How the Race's Body IS the Class
Lower Drakols generate water biologically (BonusSkill: GenerateWater). The Water Slime is a symbiotic organism that has bonded with the Drakol since birth/hatching — it feeds on their generated water and in return provides utility. The class is "direct the thing that lives on/with you." No other race generates water; no other race has this symbiote. The slime isn't a spell effect — it's a living organism that exists because of Drakol biology.

### Open Questions
- What happens if the slime "dies"? Does it regenerate from the Drakol's water generation? Is it unkillable but disableable?
- How does slime direction work as input? Pet commands? Targeting priority? Positional placement?
- Slime healing vs. traditional healing throughput — is it slower but passive (slime heals while Drakol fights) or burst-capable?
- Does the slime have its own HP pool? Can it be targeted? Is protecting the slime part of the gameplay?
- How does "healer that isn't a caster" interact with anti-magic effects? Is the Spiritcaller immune to silence?

## 5. Reaver / Deephunter

**Race:** Chunkers  
**Tier:** Experienced  
**Lock Type:** Biological (shark/piranha sensory system + aquatic adaptation = pursuit physiology)  
**Role:** DPS  

### Strategic Identity
Pursuit predator. Once committed to a kill, CANNOT disengage willingly. The Reaver is calm until the hunt begins — then unstoppable, accelerating, and locked on. Shark/piranha behavior: passive until blood in the water, then frenzy. Distinct from Nightmare (self-damage/shadow manipulation) — Reaver is PURSUIT + COMMITMENT. No self-harm mechanic, no shadow theme. This is "I smell weakness, I chase until dead."

### What It Replaces (Consumable Baseline)
Tracking items, speed potions, berserker draughts. The Reaver's biology provides tracking (bloodscent), acceleration (pursuit bonus), and damage ramping (frenzy) — all consumable categories that other classes need items for.

### Input Modality / Fantasy
Predator commitment. The fantasy is the shark — serene and efficient until prey is wounded, then relentless and terrifying. Player skill is in TARGET SELECTION (who do I commit to?) because once committed, you can't change your mind. Bad commitment = death (you can't retreat from a bad fight). Good commitment = guaranteed kill.

### Key Mechanics (Conceptual)
- **Bloodscent:** When target drops below HP threshold → Reaver gains tracking + speed bonus toward that specific target
- **Frenzy State:** Damage and speed ramp over time while pursuing — but CAN'T RETREAT. Frenzy builds the longer the chase continues
- **Pursuit Lock:** Once committed, disengagement is not voluntary. The Reaver MUST finish or be killed
- **Aquatic Terrain Advantage:** Water = bonus speed / no movement penalty. Home field advantage in aquatic environments
- Calm state → detection of weakness → commitment → acceleration → kill OR die trying

### How the Race's Body IS the Class
Chunkers have literal bloodscent — sensory organs that detect HP loss / biological distress in targets. Their bodies are built for pursuit: hydrodynamic, fast-twitch dominant, designed to accelerate toward weakened prey. Frenzy is a physiological state (adrenaline cascade, pain suppression, tunnel vision) — not a buff, not a spell, a BIOLOGICAL RESPONSE to prey weakness. Aquatic advantage is their native environment bonus. You can't teach bloodscent. You can't learn frenzy. Your body either does this or it doesn't.

### Open Questions
- How does "can't disengage" work mechanically? Movement locked toward target? Abilities only work toward target? Penalties for moving away?
- What happens if the target is healed above threshold? Does Bloodscent break? Does Frenzy drop?
- In party content, how does commitment interact with tank/healer expectations? Is the Reaver expected to peel off — and they CAN'T?
- Aquatic advantage — how much content is aquatic? Is this a strong niche or a rare bonus?
- Calm state gameplay — what does the Reaver do when NOT pursuing? Are they just a mediocre DPS until blood triggers?

## 6. Colony

**Race:** Harvesters  
**Tier:** Base  
**Lock Type:** Biological (eusocial insect biology — phenotype IS class expression)  
**Role:** Support / DPS  

### Strategic Identity
Eusocial insect biology expressed as 3 phenotypes = 3 different gameplay expressions within one class. The Colony acts like a small colony even when solo — one Harvester embodies a role within the greater organism. Every Harvester must be able to fend for themselves (GATHER role is racial/economic, separate from combat class). The phenotype determines HOW they fight, not WHETHER they fight.

### What It Replaces (Consumable Baseline)
Construction materials (Builder), scouting items (Flutter), light/trap consumables (Nightfly). Each phenotype replaces a different consumable category through biological capability.

### Input Modality / Fantasy
The fantasy varies by phenotype but the throughline is: you are one organism filling a colony role, effective alone but designed to synergize with other phenotypes. The player chooses their phenotype (permanent? per-character? per-build?) and that determines their entire gameplay loop.

### Key Mechanics (Conceptual)

**Builder Phenotype:**
- Barriers, repair, fortification — area denial through construction
- Creates physical structures that block, redirect, or protect
- The "engineer" expression — territory control through building

**Flutter Phenotype:**
- Aerial scouting, pollen CC clouds, seed-drop attacks
- Mobility + area debuff delivery
- The "skirmisher" expression — fast, disruptive, hard to pin down

**Nightfly Phenotype:**
- Bioluminescent lures, darkness navigation, ambush traps
- Vision manipulation — creates darkness for others, sees through it themselves
- The "ambush" expression — controls information and punishes those who can't see

### How the Race's Body IS the Class
Harvesters ARE eusocial insects. Their phenotype is their body — Builder caste has reinforced limbs and secretion glands for construction material. Flutter caste has wings and pollen sacs. Nightfly caste has bioluminescent organs and dark-adapted sensory systems. The phenotype isn't a spec choice — it's what KIND of Harvester you physically are. The colony structure is their biology, not their culture. One body cannot do what another phenotype's body does.

### Open Questions
- Is phenotype permanent (character creation) or selectable (per-build/per-instance)?
- How do phenotypes interact in a party of multiple Harvesters? Colony synergy bonuses?
- At Base tier, how does the 3-phenotype complexity reconcile with "Base = accessible"? Is each phenotype individually simple?
- GATHER role separation — does every Harvester have GATHER regardless of combat phenotype? Is GATHER always active?
- "Fend for themselves" — does this mean each phenotype has baseline self-sufficiency (some healing, some defense, some damage)?

## 7. Strix

**Race:** Featherin  
**Tier:** Experienced  
**Lock Type:** Biological (hollow bones + flight-capable wings + aerial physiology = altitude combat system)  
**Role:** DPS  

### Strategic Identity
EDF Wing Diver reference. Altitude as resource. Pre-calculation class: skill is in the READ from above, not execution on the ground. The player calculates cleaves/AoE BEFORE arrival (on dive initiation), resolves damage at commit time, then CANNOT LEAVE once engaged on the ground. Fragile (hollow bones, SPD 15, STR 6) — ground time is vulnerable, air time is advantaged but exposed to anti-air. The skill expression is in reading the battlefield from altitude, calculating the optimal dive, and committing perfectly.

### What It Replaces (Consumable Baseline)
Flight potions, dive-attack scrolls, reconnaissance items. The Strix's body provides altitude (flight), reconnaissance (elevated vision), and dive-attack capability (mass + velocity + fragility = glass cannon physics).

### Input Modality / Fantasy
Pre-calculation + commitment. The fantasy is the bird of prey — circling above, reading the battlefield, choosing the perfect moment, diving with lethal precision, then being vulnerable on the ground until they can re-ascend. The player's skill window is BEFORE the attack (positioning, target selection, AoE placement), not during execution. Once committed, the trajectory is fixed.

### Key Mechanics (Conceptual)
- **Altitude as Resource:** Higher = better vision + safer from melee, but costs stamina/time to maintain and exposes to anti-air
- **Pre-calculation:** Player selects AoE/cleave targets while airborne — damage is "loaded" before dive
- **Commit Dive:** Dive initiated → trajectory fixed → damage resolves on landing → Strix is grounded
- **Ground Vulnerability:** SPD 15 helps escape, but STR 6 and hollow bones = fragile if caught. Ground time must be minimized
- **Air Time Management:** Safe when airborne (from melee), exposed to anti-air. Shared class variants prioritize maintaining airtime + evasion + escaping when targeted aerial
- **Command-Fixed Movement:** Once dive trajectory is committed, cannot change direction mid-dive

### How the Race's Body IS the Class
Featherin have wings that provide TRUE FLIGHT (not gliding, not jumping high — actual sustained flight). Hollow bones make them fragile but light. The altitude combat system requires a body that can fly, hover, read from above, and dive with kinetic force. SPD 15 is their escape speed on ground (hollow bones = light = fast on foot). STR 6 is the tradeoff — light enough to fly means not strong enough to brawl. The class is "use your flight to set up devastating dives" — no wings, no class.

### Open Questions
- How does altitude work spatially? Is it abstracted (high/medium/low states) or continuous?
- Anti-air mechanics — what threatens the Strix while airborne? Ranged attacks? Specific anti-air abilities?
- Ground time duration — is there a fixed "grounded" state after dive, or can they re-launch immediately if not interrupted?
- Pre-calculation UI — how does the player "load" their dive target selection? Targeting overlay while airborne?
- Party role — is the Strix a burst DPS (dive → huge damage → vulnerable → re-ascend → repeat) or sustained (frequent shallow dives)?
- How does SPD 15 interact with ground escape? Is it fast enough to survive ground time consistently?

## 8. Wavecaller

**Race:** Jumpers  
**Tier:** Experienced  
**Lock Type:** Biological (vocal apparatus for sound propagation + water portal generation physiology)  
**Role:** Healer / DPS  

### Strategic Identity
Sound magic = fast AoE healing (vocalization, possibly no LoS requirement since sound propagates through/around obstacles). Damage = water portal generation → dive through → physically smash enemy → emerge. The aggressive healer who LEAVES the backline to deal damage (portal dive = gap close), but healing is strongest at RANGE. Constant push/pull tension between diving forward for damage vs. staying back for safe heals. NOT Jaguar from ToS (not spam-CC that forces standstills).

### What It Replaces (Consumable Baseline)
AoE healing items, teleportation scrolls, gap-close consumables. The Wavecaller's voice replaces healing items (sound = heal) and their portals replace mobility items (water portal = teleport + damage delivery).

### Input Modality / Fantasy
Aggressive healer with positional tension. The fantasy is the creature that heals by singing from safety but WANTS to dive through water portals and smash things with physical force. The player constantly decides: stay safe and heal the party optimally, or portal-dive for damage and heal less effectively. The skill expression is in managing this tension — knowing when to dive and when to sing.

### Key Mechanics (Conceptual)
- **Sound Healing:** Vocalization-based AoE healing — possibly ignores LoS (sound propagates around corners/through walls)
- **Water Portal Dive:** Generate water portal → dive through → emerge at target → physical melee damage → must return or stay exposed
- **Positional Tension:** Healing strongest at RANGE, damage requires MELEE proximity. Can't optimize both simultaneously
- **High Water Elemental Emphasis:** Water attacks, water portals, water terrain manipulation
- **Humanoid Frame:** Shylily-esque cute aesthetic, not whale-sized. Teeth are visual flavor only, not mechanical
- **Gap Close as Damage Delivery:** Portal dive IS the damage rotation — the travel is the attack

### How the Race's Body IS the Class
Jumpers have vocal apparatus that produces healing-frequency sound AND physiology that generates/interfaces with water portals. The sound healing isn't learned magic — it's what their voice physically DOES to biological tissue (resonant healing). The water portals are generated biologically (similar to Drakol water generation but expressed as portal creation). Their body is built for the dive — streamlined for portal transit, strong enough for impact damage on emergence. Teeth are present (visual identity) but the mechanical identity is voice + portal physiology.

### Open Questions
- Sound healing no-LoS — how powerful is this? Does it trivialize positioning for healing?
- Portal dive — does the Wavecaller RETURN through the portal, or must they walk/fight back? One-way or two-way?
- Positional tension — is there a middle ground (medium range = okay healing + okay damage) or is it binary (back = heal, front = damage)?
- How does "aggressive healer" interact with party expectations? Do parties want their healer diving?
- Water portal generation — resource cost? Cooldown? Can others use the portals?
- Distinction from Siren — both are voice-based healers. Siren = voice COMMANDS enemies, Wavecaller = voice HEALS allies. Is this clear enough in practice?
- How does Jumper aquatic nature interact with Chunker aquatic nature? Shared water environments?
